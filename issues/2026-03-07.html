Issue #1 March 7, 2026

The Race Heats Up: GPT-5 Benchmarks, EU AI Act Enforcement Begins

Editor's Take

This week marked a turning point in the AI industry. Leaked benchmarks for OpenAI's GPT-5 show significant gains in reasoning and multimodal tasks, but perhaps more interesting is the narrowing gap between proprietary and open-source models. The frontier is no longer the exclusive domain of well-funded labs.

Meanwhile, the EU AI Act officially entered its enforcement phase on March 1st. Companies now face real consequences for non-compliance, and the ripple effects are already visible — from updated terms of service to hastily assembled compliance teams. The regulation era of AI is no longer theoretical.

On the open-source front, DeepSeek's latest release has turned heads with performance that rivals models ten times its parameter count. Efficient architectures may be the story of 2026, not brute-force scaling. The implications for deployment costs, edge computing, and accessibility are enormous.

This Week's Top Stories

GPT-5 Benchmark Results Leaked: Reasoning Scores Jump 40%

ArXiv Research

Preliminary benchmark results attributed to OpenAI's next-generation model show a 40% improvement in multi-step reasoning tasks and near-human performance on graduate-level science exams. The results, posted anonymously to ArXiv, have not been officially confirmed but align with insider expectations.

Google DeepMind Unveils Gemini Ultra 2.0 with Real-Time Video Understanding

The Verge Industry

Google DeepMind's latest Gemini release can process and reason about live video streams in real time, opening new possibilities for robotics, surveillance, and accessibility tools. The model is available through a limited API preview starting next week.

EU AI Act Enforcement Begins: What Companies Need to Know

Reuters Policy

The European Union's AI Act moved from paper to practice this week as enforcement mechanisms went live. High-risk AI systems must now meet transparency and safety requirements, with fines up to 7% of global revenue for violations. Early guidance documents suggest regulators will focus initially on foundation model providers.

DeepSeek-V3 Punches Above Its Weight with Novel MoE Architecture

Hugging Face Open Source

DeepSeek's V3 model, using a mixture-of-experts architecture with only 16B active parameters, matches the performance of dense models five times its size on key benchmarks. Fully open-source with permissive licensing, it's already the most-downloaded model on Hugging Face this month.

The Scaling Laws Are Breaking — And That's a Good Thing

MIT Technology Review Opinion

A thought-provoking essay argues that the end of simple scaling laws forces the field toward more creative, efficient approaches. Rather than a crisis, the plateau in brute-force scaling may accelerate breakthroughs in architecture design, data curation, and training methodology.

Building a Local RAG Pipeline with DeepSeek-V3 and LangChain

Towards Data Science Tutorial

A step-by-step guide to building a retrieval-augmented generation pipeline that runs entirely on local hardware. Uses DeepSeek-V3 as the base model, ChromaDB for vector storage, and LangChain for orchestration. Includes benchmarks showing viable performance on a single RTX 4090.
